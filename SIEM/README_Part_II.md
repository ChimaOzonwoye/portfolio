## Simply Put, What SECT Is

SECT is not just for small businesses. It is for everyone. SECT is for the everyday person. Since nothing is 100 percent certain, at least from this perspective, it is certain that 99 percent of people who use their phones or computers and know how to open their emails can definitely use SECT. It is that simple. They can upload plain text, pictures/screenshots, and audio.

## When Privacy Meets Reality

The initial approach considered using normal LLM APIs, but that would not only cost more but also defeat the privacy purpose. With an LLM fine-tuned and SECT using its API like it uses other APIs such as OCR, this would be feasible. Individuals have been successfully fine-tuning models they use locally. Also, this would be better than normal LLM APIs as it would be more security-focused and trained.

The version of SECT envisioned as truly enterprise-grade involves taking an open-source LLM and fine-tuning it into a security intelligence. Since they are free, the only cost would be hosting on a cloud platform. Or better still, use the DeepSeek approach and host it on a local computer with sufficient storage, with backups in different locations like a school. A college would not mind housing this when it only costs them space and minimal electricity. After hosting on a cloud platform and inputting its API into SECT, the analysis would improve significantly. Since it is privacy-first, SECT searches this database like a user asking ChatGPT for info, but SECT will not have prompt memory after giving results. Its only memory is its knowledge database.

## Students, Professors, and Everyone Else

The student individuals do not need to be experts. As mentioned, these attacks are targeted and would not need much nuance to notice by someone who is not the intended target.

Here lies a unique opportunity. Students often seek the most efficient path and may be hesitant to volunteer without clear incentives. However, partnerships with colleges could solve two problems at once. SECT gets its human analysts, and institutions, where professors worry that students are handing assignments over to AI and losing critical thinking skills, can readjust their curricula.

Professors can introduce lab work with significant grade weight where students must verify real SECT queries. This provides students with assignments they cannot simply hand off to AI. If their assessment and resolution for the user are great, the user can grade their assistance, translating to course grades. The students get real-life experience, and the integrity of their coursework is preserved. Everyone wins.

## Beginning With What We Have

After much consideration, starting with a small parameter model seems ideal, as this would easily enable fine-tuning locally on a personal computer. If all goes well, this would be the first LLM model for SECT.
